About

I lead UX at Scentsy — designing the experiences that connect a fragrance brand with millions of customers and consultants worldwide.

I've spent my career at the intersection of research and design. I believe you can't design well without first understanding deeply — which is why usability testing and qualitative research sit at the center of everything I do, not at the end.

As UX Design Lead at Scentsy, I manage a team of designers and researchers responsible for end-to-end digital experiences across e-commerce, mobile, and internal consultant tools. I've helped move the organization from instinct-based to evidence-based design decision making.

I grew up in Kalamazoo, Michigan and have called Meridian, Idaho home since 1999. When I'm not in Figma, I'm logging miles on my Pinarello Prince somewhere outside Boise.

My Process

Research first.
Always.

01

Discover & Define

I start with the problem, not the solution. User interviews, stakeholder alignment, competitive landscape, and hard success metrics — before any wireframes exist.

02

Explore & Prototype

Divergent thinking across the full solution space, rapid low-fidelity prototypes, and early feedback loops with actual users before committing to direction.

03

Test & Iterate

Moderated usability testing with 5–8 participants per round, 2–3 rounds per project. Evidence-based iteration — not gut checks. Every design decision is traceable to a finding.

04

Deliver & Measure

Precise handoff documentation, close dev collaboration, and post-launch measurement tied to metrics set in discovery. The work isn't done when it ships — it's done when it performs.
